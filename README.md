# SME_Manufacturing_Database_1000 

Project Title: Strategic Database of 1000 Indian Manufacturing SMEs

1. Objective: To identify and categorize 1,000 Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) in the Indian manufacturing sector with an annual revenue range of ₹50Cr to ₹500Cr.

2. Research Methodology (The Process):

Clustering Approach: Instead of random searching, I used an Industrial Hub Clustering method. I focused on major manufacturing belts like Pune (Auto), Bangalore (Aerospace/Tech), NCR (Electronics), and Ahmedabad (Engineering).

Sector Diversification: The database covers diverse sectors including Automotive parts, Aerospace components, Textile machinery, Electrical equipment, and Industrial Automation.

3. Data Points Captured:

Company Name

Primary Industry/Sector

Key Manufacturing Location (City)

Estimated Revenue Bracket (₹50Cr - ₹500Cr)

4. Scientific Execution Logic:
Following the DeepThought philosophy, I treated this data collection as an observation phase. The goal was to understand the "Execution Gap" in these SMEs and how the PDGMS AI Platform could potentially help them scale from ₹100Cr to ₹500Cr+ by optimizing their business processes.
